The Quest to bring repeat cinema goers to IMDB

  • GA Student Project
  • Duration: 2 weeks
  • Date Completed: Sept-2014

Challenge

IMDb is a popular film information and review’s website with almost 3 million film and TV titles and over 55 million registered users.

In this case study, they decide to expand their market from being an information provider to selling cinema tickets directly in the UK. They want a service that:

  • Informs you of of movies showing at cinemas in your area
  • Allows you to easily purchase tickets
  • Uses a digital pass to use at the cinema

My Role

Working with a team of UX Designers over two weeks, we interviewed cinema goers and researched the opportunities for IMDB through competitive analysis. User Research

Personas

Our screening survey of over 50 responsents enabled us to find and interview 8 cinema goers:

  • Age Range: 28-41
  • identified as infrequent cinema goers
  • All had bought cinema tickets online previously

Key Insights

  • People are familiar with the IMDB site and brand, but used the site infrequently.
  • Friend recommendations was ranked the 2nd most popular reason for choosing to see a film, above who was in it and what critics say. The most popular influencer was the film trailer or poster.
  • Few people identified online ticket purchasing as needing improvement, but this was a small proportion out of other needs talked about.

Solution: Just Add Button?

Our research and interviews identified that IMDb’s proposition to sell cinema tickets alone was unlikely to meet the needs of UK cinema goers. Currently users perception of IMDb is firmly as an information provider, firstly of film facts and trivia, followed by some trust of reviews, but then little to no awareness of the showtime listings service.

Users are not using the site to meet the pre-curosor steps before deciding to purchse a cinema ticket. Our hypothesis therefore is that if we focused solely on adding and designing a purchasing feature, this would not be sufficient to capture the majority of cinema goers.

We needed to tackle the needs higher up the funnel in order to bring people to IMDb to first disocver the films they want to see and help them in planning their trip, before direciing them to purchase tickets.

Design Studio
Method

The Design Studio Method involves quickly sketching, critiquing and building on ideas in a group workshop.

Using our personas and research we created more than a dozen different ideas in the course of a 2 hour session and then sorted and prioritised these into:

Must Do

  • Map and search tool to help locate cinema based on location
  • Smartphone app to cater for tech savvy, but keep consistent across different devices and website
  • Provide a digital pass, like QR code saved within the app, so its the right size for scanning
  • Quicker checkout by integrating with common payment options, e.g. Amazon and Paypal

Won't Do

  • Automatic plays tailer when app scans a film poster
  • Loyalty scheme for ticket purhcases
  • On-boarding process that let’s you choose which critics you trust
  • Prompt after seeing a film to rate reviewers

Should Do

  • Save a seat for a friend without having to pay for their ticket
  • Get your friends opinion on whether to see a film
  • Keyword search and filtered categories to customise what films to see

Could Do

  • Browser plugin to feed into IMDb watchlist
  • Present offers and events, such as cheaper tickets and special showings
